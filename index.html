Badminton

Badminton is a popular racket sport played by two (singles) or four players (doubles) on a rectangular court divided by a net. It involves hitting a shuttlecock over the net with rackets, aiming to make it land within the opponent's half of the court. Badminton is known for being a fast-paced and physically demanding sport, requiring agility, speed, and precision . Key aspects of badminton: Court Dimensions: The badminton court is rectangular, typically 44 feet long and 17 feet wide for singles, and 20 feet wide for doubles. Scoring: Matches are usually the best of three games, with each game played to 21 points. Shuttlecock: The shuttlecock, also known as a birdie, is a lightweight object with a feathered or plastic skirt. Equipment: The main equipment includes rackets and the shuttlecock. Rules: Key rules include serving underhand, hitting the shuttlecock before it bounces, and changing ends after each game.
